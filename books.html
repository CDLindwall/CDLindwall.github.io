Before my love of newspapers and magazines, I started first with a love simply of words. I wasn't really a writer, but I was a reader.

Books, the ones that stick, show everything that's possible with language. They show what's possible with creativity, talent, and a knack for storytelling. I believe that if you want to become a writer, there has to be an appreciation of your literary heritage. For that reason, I still read and read often. I believe my own writing (professionally and otherwise) is so intrinsically tied to the books that I've clung to, the authors that have inspired me, and the characters that I've loved. For that reason, I love sharing my own influences.

"Creative Nonfiction" is my favorite genre. I've always enjoyed it personally, but have also recently taken an interest in it professionally, as well. This style expresses itself in anything from a magazine feature story to the "true crime" classic In Cold Blood to a personal memoir. The blending of reporting and artistic language is powerful. I believe it stays true to way human beings have always shared stories. We don't just give facts. We create narrative arcs and dramatic unveilings, suspenseful cliffhangers and moving finales.

Stories have emotion -- creative language gives them life.
